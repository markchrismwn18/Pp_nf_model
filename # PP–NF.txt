# PP–NF Unified Model

**Pressure-Phase / Null-Field Framework**  
A unified two-component ontological model for physics from atomic to cosmological scales.

## Overview

The PP–NF framework posits that spacetime is exclusively occupied by two mutually exclusive components: **PP** (positive-pressure, compressive) and **NF** (null-field, expansive). These components obey strict **non-co-occupancy** — they cannot share the same spatial volume — and interact solely through **force-density conservation**:

\[
x + y = |1|
\]

where \(x\) is the fractional PP force density and \(y\) is the fractional NF displacement. Their opposition drives a **volumetric oscillation** (volume swing), which serves as the sole dynamical degree of freedom of the system.

This single mechanism provides a unified description of:
- Atomic structure and chemical periodicity
- Galactic rotation curves
- Cosmological expansion and total energy budget
- Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB) acoustic spectrum

without invoking dark matter halos, a separate cosmological constant, or an inflaton field.

- **N40 Hierarchy Bridge**: Dimensionless ratio \(N_{40} = 4.3 \times 10^{40}\) derived from CODATA constants linking atomic-scale equilibria (Penning-trap electron compression, Bohr radius) to the observable universe horizon. This bridge closes the cosmological energy ledger:
  \[
  E_{\text{osc}} = E_{\text{sym}} = \rho_{\text{crit}} V c^2
  \]
  via the red-shifted swing frequency \(\omega = \omega_0 / N_{40}\), where \(\omega_0 = c / a_0\).

- **Oscillating Underlay**: Classical standing-wave displacement field in the NF elastic medium, described by spherical Bessel functions \(R_{n\ell}(r) \propto j_\ell(k_{n\ell} r)\). This underlay manifests as:
  - Atomic orbitals (s, p, d, f shapes from spherical harmonics \(Y_{\ell m}\))
  - Revised periodic table: radial fan geometry centered on Fe-56 (optimal PP–NF equilibrium)
  - Galactic dip + wall stiffness \(S(r)\)
  - CMB acoustic resonances

- **Single Global Parameter**: \(\alpha\) (or \(\Delta_{PN}\)), calibrated on the SPARC rotation-curve database. This parameter simultaneously governs wall stiffness, environmental R_dip scaling, cosmic swing amplitude, and CMB power spectrum amplitudes.

- **Environment Dependence**: NF expansion is fastest in PP-poor regions, naturally producing higher local expansion rates in voids and suppressed late-time clustering — consistent with 2026 DESI DR2, DES Y6, and KBC void analyses.
